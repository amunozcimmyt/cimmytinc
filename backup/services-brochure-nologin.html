CTAB DNA extraction for seed or grain (SAGA)

At SAGAs Lab, DNA isolation is performed using multiple tailored strategies to accommodate both fresh and preserved tissue types. This service begins with the receipt of seed or grain samples, which are germinated in a controlled greenhouse to produce uniform seedlings. Young leaf tissue is harvested at the optimal growth stage and processed using a CTAB (Cetyltrimethylammonium Bromide) protocol, which effectively removes polysaccharides and secondary metabolites to yield high-quality DNA. The extraction workflow is designed to produce DNA suitable for downstream applications such as sequencing, marker-assisted selection, and quality assurance.

As part of the service, SAGAs Lab develops a detailed output report for each sample, including the Sample ID and DNA quality metrics. This ensures traceability, reproducibility, and confidence in the genomic material delivered for research and breeding applications.

Key Strengths and Applications

  • ✓ Highly effective for plant tissues rich in polysaccharides and secondary metabolites.
  • ✓ Cost-efficient and adaptable for diverse species.
  • ✓ Suitable for both seed and leaf tissue.

Pricing

DArTseq has different genotyping options, so pricing depends on sequencing depth and the marker-density desired by the user. The options are the following: · DArTseq High-Density, 2 Millon reads ; 45 USD/sample · DArTseq Mid-Density, 1.5 Millon reads ; 35 USD/sample · DArTseq Low-Density, 0.8 Millon reads ; 25 USD/sample

Turnaround time

Results will be delivered on the platform within 1-2 weeks from the start of sample processing.

DArTag: Cost-effective, low-density genotyping (SAGA)

DArTag is a high-throughput, targeted genotyping solution that delivers flexible, accurate, and cost-effective results across a wide range of plant species. Whether you are mapping traits or accelerating breeding, DArTag is designed to empower your research.

Unlike DArT’s various traditional genotyping platforms, DArTag is not limited to markers identified by the DArTseq system. It enables the targeting of any SNP or small indel, provided there is some genomic sequence available around the variant. This opens the door to highly customizable panels tailored to specific traits, populations, or research goals.

The DArTag workflow involves two key steps:

  • Target Selection: Specially designed molecular probes capture small genomic regions containing the desired sequence variants.
  • Amplification and Barcoding: These regions are amplified via PCR while simultaneously attaching a unique sample-specific barcode.

resulting libraries are sequenced using next-generation sequencing (NGS) platforms. DArT’s proprietary bioinformatics pipeline then processes the data, delivering high-quality and reproducible genotyping results.
